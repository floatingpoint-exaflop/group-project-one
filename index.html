War... What is it good for?

Rules of War

The deck is divided equally among the two players, with each player receiving a face-down stack

Players simultaneously flip the top card of their deck, also known as a "battle"

The player with the higher card takes both cards and puts them face down at the bottom of their stack

If the cards are the same rank, it's "war"

Both players place the next card from their pile face down and then another card face up

The player with the higher face-up card wins the war and adds all the cards on the table to the bottom of their deck

If players flip over cards of the same rank, a "war" is played

During a "war", each player turns up one card face down and one card face up

The player with the higher cards takes both piles (six cards)

If a player runs out of cards in the middle of a "war", they lose the war and are out of the game

Alternatively, they can turn their last card face up, and these count as their played card in the war

The game continues until one player wins all the cards from the table

History of War

War is a classic card game with origins dating back to ancient times, although there is no actual origination date. It a timeless and universally loved source of entertainment for people of all ages and cultures.
